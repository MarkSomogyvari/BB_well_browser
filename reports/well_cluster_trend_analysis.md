## Key Observations

### 1. High-Decline Hotspots
- **Clusters 18, 16, and 19** exhibit the most severe groundwater declines, exceeding **2.5 cm/year**.
- Cluster 18, in particular, shows a median decline of 2.64 cm/year and is 100% significant, indicating a very consistent and rapid drop in levels.

### 2. Large-Scale Stability
- **Cluster 14**, the largest cluster containing 486 wells (approx. 38% of the high-quality dataset), shows a more moderate decline of **-0.62 cm/year**. This cluster likely represents the broad regional background trend.

### 3. Outliers and Variability
- **Cluster 24** shows a positive mean trend but a negative median. The very high standard deviation (12.8 cm/year) suggests that a few wells with massive increases (potentially due to local management or data errors) are skewing the average.

## Conclusion
The groundwater decline is not uniform across the study area. Certain regions (represented by Clusters 18 and 16) are experiencing significantly faster depletion of surface-near aquifers than the regional average. Identifying the climatic or anthropogenic drivers specific to these clusters will be a priority for the modeling phase.
